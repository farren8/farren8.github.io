high falls recreation site

blushing from sun from cold streams + campfire from getting in + getting out hurting + being hurting to be the frogs in the pond that little girl told us about "i can cross the stream in 5 seconds" we watched her i can too, but i don't tell her to be those little frogs spotted + striped, floating eyes open do they know it's spring? maybe they do because we watch them chase each other around that little pond fuck and then move on maybe spring can be that simple

april 3

cats tread softly on palms left open do not wait to hold out a holding hand do not wait for the fruit to bruise like knees unwashed like rubber boots with souls soaking to grow as moss on tree is still growing to fly as kite on string is still flying to be tethered is no dying to be tethered is to be free

this summer

i want to be freckled, tanned eat greek yogurt, figs and honey drink water with cucumber and mint like those girls do. i want to be barefoot in an unfamiliar grocery store bite the peach before i buy it stagger blindly back into the sun. to be nauseous, sunstroked and blushing ribs poking up into the sky stretching like a cat in the sand. and to have you there beside me to gather my ribs into your hands to touch my back underwater as the waves fill our mouths - your salty lips smiling into mine. i wanted that.

pray for me

if you pray for me, i'll pray for you it's morning. i hide under the sheets, you are running around collecting your things. i watch you dress: underwear, jeans, t-shirt, sweater, socks. i want to take it all off of you again tonight, and the next night, and the night after that. you sit on the edge of the bed and look at me bundling the sheets to hide my nakedness, forgetting not a moment ago - our bodies were as two palms press into each other praying.

apple core (for natalie)

leave but the seeds and stem for as the buffalo grass thins and narrows for sky my love has limits i thin, i dry with the coming of fall i wrap and wall a tethered root bosom of thy fruit - a core's core the seeds, the stem the last wish i couldn't send planted in the morning dew for what's me is me what's you is you to let you start anew. now children play beneath your tree and a lover sits patiently for the fruit to fall and the dove to call for i left but the seeds and stem
